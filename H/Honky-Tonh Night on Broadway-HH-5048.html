Honk-Tonk Night On Broadway    (HH 5048)   Ernie Kinney

OPENER - MIDDLE BREAK - ENDING



All 4 ladies promenade go once inside the ring
Get back and swing with your man
Join hands, circle to the left around and then
Left allemande and weave the ring
I'm hanging in by hanging out, so I'll dance the night away
Dosado and promenade I say
I'm tired of sleeping all alone, my arms can't reach to San Antone
Tonight's another honky-tonk night on Broadway

FIGURE



Head couples promenade go halfway round the ring
Come down the middle and do a right & left thru
Square thru I say 4 hands around that way
Dosado with that corner Sue, curlique
Scoot back and the boys fold, the girls turn thru
Star thru and promenade go walking round you do
I'm tired of sleeping all alone, my arms can't reach to San Antone
Tonight's another honky-tonk night on Broadway

ALTERNATE FIGURE



1 & 3 you promenade go halfway round the ring
Come down the middle and do a curlique, boys run
Go right & left thru the outside 2 and then dive thru
Pass thru and do the right & left thru, turn the girls
Star thru, flutter wheel, straight across you go
Reverse your flutter, take this girl and then you promeno
I'm tired of sleeping all alone, my arms can't reach to San Antone
Tonight's another honky-tonk night on Broadway

ALTERNATE FIGURE PLUS



1 & 3 lead to the right, veer to the left and then
Bend the line, and do the right & left thru
Pass thru, do a U-turn back, load the boat and then
Move right aong my friend and when you do, turn thru
Allemande left with the corner, come home a dosado
Swing the corner girl and promenade
I'm tired of sleeping all alone, my arms can't reach to San Antone
Tonight's another honky-tonk night on Broadway

ALTERNATE FIGURE ADVANCED



Heads square thru 4 hands around you go
Meet the corner with a dosado
Swing thru and then, those boys run and roll you do
Girls tag each other then star thru
Cast a shadow all the way thru and then
Swing thru my friend, step thru,
swing the corner, promenade you do
I'm tired of sleeping all alone, my arms can't reach to San Antone
