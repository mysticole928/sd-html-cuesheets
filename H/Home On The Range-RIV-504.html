Home On The Range
Vernon Dalhart; Bing Crosby; many others 1927
(Riverboat 504 X) Elmer Sheffield, Jr. , Max Ringe
OPENER

Sides face, grand square
*Oh give me a home where the buffalo roam
And the deer and the antelope play
Where seldom is heard a discouraging word
And the skies are not cloudy all day
Allemande and weave
Home, home on the range
Meet your girl and promenade home
Where seldom is heard a discouraging word
And the skies are not cloudy all day

ALTERNATE LYRICS

*How often at night, when the heavens are bright
With the lights from the glittering stars
Have I stood there amazed, and asked as I gaze
If their glory exceeds that of ours

*Where the air is so pure and the zephyrs are free
The breeze so balmy and light
I would not exchange my home on the range
For all of the cities so bright

FIGURE

Heads promenade go halfway around
Lead right and do a dosado
Swing thru tonight then boys run right
Bend the line and a right & left thru
Touch 1/4 now, then all circulate
Boys run right , swing and you promenade
Where seldom is heard a discouraging word
And the skies are not cloudy all day
