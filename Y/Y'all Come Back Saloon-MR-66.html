Y'all Come Back Saloon    (MR 66)   Tom Trainor

OPENER



Sides face, grand square
She played tambourine with a silver jingle
And she must have know the words to a million tunes
But the one most requested by the one she knew as cowboy
Was the late night benediction at the y'all come back saloon
All four ladies promenade once around the ring
Swing your man and promenade around the square
And all the fallen angels and pinball playing rounders
Stopped the games that they were playing for the
Losers evening prayer

FIGURE



Heads (sides) square thru four hands around you go
Meet the sides (heads) and make a right hand star and then
Heads (sides) star left in the middle once around you go
Meet the outside pair and swing thru again
Don't stop, spin the top, slide thru you know
Swing the corner lady, promenade the room
The tune most requested by the one she knew as cowboy
Was the late night benediction at the y'all come back saloon

MIDDLE BREAK



Circle left
Faded love and faded memory, how they lingered in her mind
Allemande left and then dosado
Allemande that corner weave the ring
Every night in the shadows, thinking back on Amarillo
Swing the corner lady, promenade the room
The tune most requested by the one she knew as cowboy
Was the late night benediction at the y'all come back saloon

CLOSER



Sides face, grand square
Faded love and faded memory, how they lingered in her mind
Smiles and tears played the cowboy like an old melody
Out of touch and out of time, four ladies promenade
Every night in the shadows, thinking back on Amarillo
Swing your lady, promenade around the room
The tune most requested by the one she knew as cowboy
Was the late night benediction at the y'all come back saloon
