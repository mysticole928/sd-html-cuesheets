Sam The Fiddlin' Man

Heads face, grand square
Old Sam was a fiddlin' man, he played the violin
He never wore no shoes, and he packed the dancers in
His style was smooth and silky as we danced around the floor
Head ladies center , tea cup chain and go
I still recall that fiddler layin' them hoedowns down
We danced the old red barn and we shook the rafters down
Well, we danced 'em all and had a ball and we always yell "one more"
As we watched them bare feet slappin' time on that old wood floor
