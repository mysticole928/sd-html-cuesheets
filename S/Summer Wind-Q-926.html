Summer Wind
Wayne Newton; Bobby Vinton; Perry Como; Frank Sinatra; others 1965
(Quadrille 926) Guy Adams
OPENER

Sides face grand square
The summer wind, came blowin' in from across the sea
It lingered there to touch your hair and walk with me
Allemande left and you weave
All summer long we sang a song
Swing and promenade
Two sweethearts and the summer wind

FIGURE

Heads square thru 4 you know
Make a right hand star
Heads star left one time you bet
Do the right & left thru
Swing thru and then, swing thru again
Swing and promenade
Two sweethearts and the summer wind

MIDDLE BREAK

Sides face grand square
Like painted kites, those days and nights, they went flyin' by
The world was new beneath a blue umbrella sky
Allemande left and weave
Then softer than a piper man
Swing and promenade
I lost you to the summer wind

CLOSER

Sides face grand square
The autumn wind and the winter winds, they have come and gone
And still the days, those lonely days, they go on and on
Allemande left and weave
And guess who sighs his lullabies through nights that never end
My fickle friend, the summer wind
