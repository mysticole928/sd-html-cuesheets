My Pretty Girl / Marching Through Georgia
Jay Ungar; others 1865
(Windsor 4412 side B ) Bruce Johnson
FIGURE

The first ol' lady promenade the inside of that ring
Then hurry home and give your man a great big healthy swing
End your swing a-facin' out, the others fall in behind
Ladies go right, gents go left and you bring 'em down in a line
The first old couple dosado , you dosado your own
The second couple dosado , you dosado your own
The next old couple dosado , you dosado your own
Last old couple dosado , you dosado your own
Now everybody forward and back , I'll tell you the reason why
Forward again and pass thru there, then look her in the eye
Step right up and swing her home, you're swing in' mighty high
That's the way we do it down in Georgia

BREAK

Allemande left your corner, and a right hand round your own
Allemande left the corner again, around the ring you roam
When you meet your partner, boys, you balance with that girl
Then right & left, continue on , go all around the world
Meet your little southern belle, you twirl and promenade
Go home to your Magnolia tree and swing there in the shade
Now step right up and don't be shy, you swing 'em mighty high
'Cause that's the way we do it down in Georgia

(Repeat figure with second gent leading out; repeat break;
Reply the record with third lady and then fourth gent leading out)

Double-sided instrumental record is on Windsor #4112 .
