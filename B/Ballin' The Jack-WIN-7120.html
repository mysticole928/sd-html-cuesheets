Bill Bailey / Ballin' The Jack
Al Jolson and Eddie Cantor; The Chordettes; many others 1913
   (Windsor 7120 side B )   
 
OPENER   

Honor your partners, the corners all, now wave to the gal across the hall
Join up hands in a great big ring, circle to the left like everything
Sashay round with the corner maid, now take your honey and you promenade
Git along home with your lady fair, no time to swing, just stop right there
Allemande left with the corners all, a right & left grand, go round the hall
Ya pick 'em up and ya lay 'em down, meet your honey and you promenade her round
Promenade home with the dear ol' thing, and when you get to your place in the ring
You dosado, go back to back, then you all get set to ball the Jack

FIGURE   

1st and 3rd stand back to back, boompsa daisy go round that track
All the way round that ring, you two, while the side two couples do a right & left thru
Right on by your own you go, to the corner couples and you line up four
Go forward eight and back with you, now forward eight and you pass right thru
Turn to the left, go single file, make a left hand wheel and spin it awhile
Gents reach back with your right hand,m now pull her through, then let 'er stand
Swing with the corner, ya gotta new pard, promenade her home to your own back yard
Hustle on home in the same old track, no time to swing when you get back

MIDDLE BREAK - CLOSER   

Ladies to the center and back to the ring, now everybody dance and everybody swing
Join up hands in a great big ring, circle to the left like everything
Sashay round with the corner maid, now take your honey and you promenade
Too dog-gone bad, it's the same old thing, you just ain't got no time to swing
Do an allemande left with the old left hand, partner by the right, go right & left grand
Hand over hand til you meet your maid, take her by the hand and you promenade
*Promenade home with the tired old thing, and when you get to your place in the ring
*You dosado, go back to back, get set once more to ball the Jack

ALTERNATE LYRICS

*Promenade home with your powder puff, I reckon she's balled the Jack enough
Swing 'er mac, don't break her back, and that's the end of "Ballin' The Jack"
